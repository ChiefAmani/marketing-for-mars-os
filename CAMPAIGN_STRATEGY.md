# CAMPAIGN_STRATEGY.md

## Project Name: MARS OS Social Media Marketing Campaign

## 1. Product/Service Overview and Unique Value Proposition
**Product:** MARS OS (formerly "Exterior Cleaning Profit Calculator") - an automated Excel-based Profit Per Job Calculator specifically for residential exterior cleaning businesses (window, gutter, siding, soft washing, and pressure washing).
**Unique Value Proposition:** "Stop guessing your prices. Get mathematical certainty on every quote, eliminate unprofitable jobs, and instantly know your true bottom line." It transforms pricing from an emotional, fear-based decision into a data-driven, confident decision. It's a one-time investment that delivers immediate ROI, positioned between free DIY spreadsheets and expensive monthly SaaS.

## 2. Target Audience Personas
**Persona 1: "The Hustling Founder - Emily"**
*   **Demographics:** 28-35 years old, female, runs a growing exterior cleaning business (1-3 employees), likely uses a smartphone for most business operations.
*   **Pain Points:** Overwhelmed by pricing, fears undercharging, struggles to account for all costs (drive time, chemicals, callbacks), tired of expensive CRM subscriptions for basic features. Wants to grow but feels stuck on profitability.
*   **Online Behavior:** Active on Instagram (stories, reels for business tips), Facebook groups for small business owners/exterior cleaners, watches YouTube tutorials, uses LinkedIn for professional networking and industry insights.

**Persona 2: "The Indie Hacker Operator - Mark"**
*   **Demographics:** 30-45 years old, male, solo owner-operator or very small team, highly analytical, values efficiency and data. Might have a background in another field before starting his business.
*   **Pain Points:** Frustrated by manual calculations, wants to optimize every aspect of his business, seeks tools that provide clear ROI, wary of recurring subscription costs. Wants to understand true profit margins on every job.
*   **Online Behavior:** Engages with Twitter threads on business growth and tools, reads industry blogs, participates in niche online forums (e.g., Pressure Washing Resource), uses LinkedIn for thought leadership and connecting with suppliers/peers.

## 3. Competitor Analysis
**Who else targets this audience:**
*   **Generic Business Calculators/Spreadsheets:** Often free or low-cost, but lack industry-specific detail.
*   **Expensive CRMs (Jobber, Housecall Pro):** Offer comprehensive solutions but are often overkill and costly for basic job costing, leading to "subscription fatigue."
*   **Other Exterior Cleaning Business Coaches/Consultants:** Offer advice, but not a direct tool for pricing.

**What content do they post:**
*   Generic calculators: Focus on broad business finance, not niche-specific.
*   CRMs: Highlight all-in-one features, scheduling, invoicing, customer management.
*   Coaches: Share tips, success stories, motivational content.

**MARS OS Differentiator:** "Built by an exterior cleaner, for exterior cleaners." Speaks their language (SH ratios, soft wash setups, surface cleaner times). One-time investment, immediate ROI.

## 4. Platform Strategy

*   **Instagram:**
    *   **Why:** High visual engagement, strong for short-form video (Reels) and quick tips (Stories, Carousels). Ideal for demonstrating the "Are You Undercharging?" reality check visually.
    *   **Best Practices:** High-quality visuals, engaging Reels with text overlays, interactive stories (polls, Q&A), clear calls to action in bio and captions.
*   **Twitter (X):**
    *   **Why:** Excellent for sharing quick insights, engaging in industry discussions, and building thought leadership through threads. Good for analytical "indie hacker" persona.
    *   **Best Practices:** Concise tweets, compelling threads breaking down pricing concepts, linking to valuable resources, using relevant hashtags, engaging with other industry accounts.
*   **LinkedIn:**
    *   **Why:** Professional networking, B2B focus. Ideal for establishing credibility, sharing in-depth articles, and connecting with business owners.
    *   **Best Practices:** Professional tone, long-form posts, articles on business profitability, sharing success stories, engaging with comments, connecting with potential beta users.
*   **Email Drip Sequence (for Beta Users):**
    *   **Why:** Direct communication channel for nurturing leads, providing detailed information, and guiding users through the beta signup process.
    *   **Best Practices:** Clear subject lines, personalized content, strong CTAs, educational value, testimonials (if available), clear instructions for beta access.

## 5. Messaging Pillars
1.  **Profit Certainty:** Eliminate guesswork, know your true margins.
2.  **Cost Control:** Uncover hidden costs (callbacks, drive time, chemicals).
3.  **Empowerment & Growth:** Take control of your business finances, scale confidently.
4.  **Niche Expertise:** Built by an industry insider, for your specific needs.
5.  **Value Over Subscription:** One-time investment, lasting ROI.

## 6. Content Types Per Platform

*   **Instagram:**
    *   **Reels:** "Are you undercharging?" calculator demos, quick pricing tips, before/after profit scenarios.
    *   **Carousels:** Step-by-step guides on using specific features (e.g., Callback Cost Quantifier), "5 Ways You're Losing Money on Every Job."
    *   **Stories:** Polls ("Do you know your true profit margin?"), Q&A sessions, behind-the-scenes of business operations.
    *   **Image Posts:** Infographics on profit metrics, testimonials (once available).
*   **Twitter (X):**
    *   **Threads:** Deep dives into pricing strategies, breaking down hidden costs, "The True Cost of a Callback" analysis.
    *   **Text Posts:** Quick tips, industry stats, questions to spark engagement.
    *   **Image/GIFs:** Visuals to support threads, humorous takes on pricing struggles.
*   **LinkedIn:**
    *   **Long-form Posts:** Case studies (hypothetical initially), articles on financial literacy for small businesses, thought leadership on sustainable growth.
    *   **Video:** Short interviews (if possible), product demos.
    *   **Document Posts:** Sharing excerpts from the "Pricing Cheat Sheet" PDF.
*   **Email Drip Sequence:**
    *   **Email 1 (Welcome/Introduction):** Introduce MARS OS, highlight core problem it solves.
    *   **Email 2 (Value Proposition):** Deep dive into a key feature (e.g., Callback Cost Quantifier), show ROI.
    *   **Email 3 (Testimonial/Social Proof):** (Once available) Share success stories.
    *   **Email 4 (Call to Action):** Clear instructions for beta signup, benefits of early access.
    *   **Email 5 (Follow-up/FAQ):** Address common questions, reiterate value.

## 7. Posting Cadence

*   **Instagram:** 3-5 posts/week (mix of Reels, Carousels, Stories).
*   **Twitter (X):** 5-7 tweets/week (including 1-2 threads).
*   **LinkedIn:** 2-3 posts/week (mix of long-form and shorter updates).
*   **Email Drip:** 1 email every 2-3 days for the beta sequence.

## 9. Tone of Voice Guidelines
*   **Authoritative & Knowledgeable:** Position MARS OS as the expert solution.
*   **Empathetic & Relatable:** Acknowledge the pain points of owner-operators.
*   **Direct & Pragmatic:** Focus on tangible benefits and ROI.
*   **Confident & Empowering:** Inspire users to take control of their finances.
*   **Slightly Edgy/Challenging:** Use phrases like "Are you undercharging?" to grab attention.
